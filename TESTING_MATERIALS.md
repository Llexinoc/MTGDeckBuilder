# ManaForge Think-Aloud Testing Materials

---

## INTRO SCRIPT (Read to Tester)

### Welcome & Overview
"Hi [Tester Name], thank you so much for taking the time to participate in this user testing session today. We're going to be looking at an application called **ManaForge — a themed Magic: The Gathering deckbuilder**. 

Before we get started, I want to walk you through how this session will work, because it's a bit different from what you might be used to when using software normally.

### Recording Permission
First, I need to ask for your permission: **I'll be recording this session using [Zoom/screen recording software]. The recording will only be used to help me understand how people interact with the app and identify areas for improvement. Your identity will be kept private, and you can ask me to stop recording at any time. Is that okay with you?**

[Wait for confirmation]

### The Think-Aloud Process
Throughout this session, I'm going to ask you to **think out loud** — meaning I want you to verbally share what you're thinking, feeling, and observing as you use the application. 

For example, you might say things like:
- "I'm looking for a button to click..."
- "I expected to see X, but I see Y instead..."
- "I'm not sure what this means..."
- "This is confusing because..."

**This is not a test of YOU.** There are no wrong answers. I'm testing the application, not your ability to use it. If you can't figure something out, that's valuable information for me — it means the app needs to be clearer.

I might ask you questions like:
- "What are you thinking right now?"
- "What do you see on the screen?"
- "What were you expecting to see?"

Feel free to speak up anytime if you're confused or if something feels odd to you.

### Preliminary Questions (Before We Start)
I'd like to ask you a few quick questions to get to know your experience level with Magic: The Gathering and deckbuilding:

1. **Have you played Magic: The Gathering before?** If yes, how long? If no, is this your first exposure?
2. **Have you ever built a Magic deck yourself?** What format(s)?
3. **When you think about building a deck, what's usually the hardest part for you?** (e.g., picking cards, balancing mana curve, knowing the meta, etc.)
4. **Have you used any online deckbuilding tools before?** (Moxfield, Archidekt, etc.) What was your experience?

[Listen to their answers — this helps you understand their baseline knowledge]

### What We'll Be Doing
Today I'm going to present you with a few different scenarios — basically, I'll describe a situation and a goal you're trying to accomplish. Your job is to use the ManaForge app to complete that goal. I'll be here observing, but I won't give you instructions on how to use the app. Just do what feels natural to you.

Does this all make sense? Do you have any questions before we start?

## APP OVERVIEW (Brief Pitch)

**ManaForge** is an AI-assisted Magic: The Gathering deckbuilder that lets you describe a deck theme in plain English and generates a complete, legally formatted deck. 

Key features:
- **Theme-based building**: Describe your deck idea (e.g., "Blue control with card draw") and the app builds it
- **Customizable constraints**: Choose deck format (Commander or Standard), power level bracket, and add reference cards
- **Instant feedback**: See card distribution, mana curve, and deck reasoning in real-time
- **No copying**: Decks are generated using deckbuilding logic, not copied from existing lists

The goal is to make deckbuilding faster and more accessible — whether you're a new player who doesn't know which cards to pick, or an experienced player who wants to explore a theme quickly.

## SCENARIOS (Read these to the Tester)

### Scenario 1: Build Your First Themed Deck

**Context & Story:**
"You're new to Magic and you've always loved dragons. Your friend told you that you could build a dragon-themed commander deck, but you have no idea where to start — there are 30,000+ cards and you don't know which dragons are good or how to balance them.

You decide to try ManaForge to see if it can help you build a dragon deck without having to learn all the card rules first."

**Goal:**
Use ManaForge to build a dragon-themed Commander deck. Once the deck is generated, spend a moment looking at what it created for you.

**Tester reads scenario aloud. Then you say:** "Go ahead and interact with the app to build this deck. I'll be watching and listening to your thoughts as you go. Remember to think out loud — tell me what you're doing and what you're noticing."

---

### Scenario 2: You Want a More Casual Deck

**Context & Story:**
"The dragon deck you just built looks cool, but it feels pretty competitive — lots of expensive, powerful cards. You remember your friend mentioning that there's a way to adjust how serious/casual a deck is.

You want to build a dragon deck that's more casual and jank-y — the kind of deck where fun plays matter more than winning, because you're playing with friends just for fun."

**Goal:**
Using the app, build a dragon deck again, but this time adjust it to be more casual/jank. See if you can tell a difference between this version and the previous one.

**Tester reads scenario aloud. Then you say:** "Go ahead and make that adjustment in the app. Think out loud as you do."

---

### Scenario 3: Add Your Favorite Card

**Context & Story:**
"You have a dragon card you absolutely love from a set you recently bought. You want to make sure it's in your new deck. You've heard some apps let you tell them 'I want this card in my deck' before building.

You want to build a blue control deck, but you want to make sure it includes a specific card you have."

**Goal:**
Build a blue control deck while adding a reference card (any card name you choose, or I can suggest one like "Counterspell" or "Mystical Tutor"). See if the app incorporates your preference.

**Tester reads scenario aloud. Then you say:** "Try adding a reference card before or while building a blue control deck. Let me know what you think happens."

---

### Scenario 4: Understand Why the Deck Was Built This Way

**Context & Story:**
"You've built a few decks now and they look interesting, but you're curious — why did ManaForge choose these specific cards? What's the logic behind the deck structure?

You want to understand the reasoning so you can learn how deckbuilding works."

**Goal:**
Look at the deck details/reasoning section and explain back to me: What is this deck doing? Why does it have the cards it has?

**Tester reads scenario aloud. Then you say:** "Explore the deck details and tell me what you think the deck is trying to do. Don't worry if you don't understand everything — just share what you see and what it means to you."

---

### Scenario 5: Switch to a Smaller Format

**Context & Story:**
"Your playgroup usually plays Commander (100-card singleton), but next week you're going to a local game store for their Friday Night Magic event, which uses Standard (60-card constructed).

You want to quickly adapt your dragon deck concept to work in this smaller, faster format."

**Goal:**
Using the same theme (dragons), build a Standard/60-card deck instead of Commander. Compare how it feels different.

**Tester reads scenario aloud. Then you say:** "Try building a dragon deck in Standard instead of Commander. Describe what you notice is different about this deck."

## QUESTIONS TO ASK DURING TESTING

If the tester gets stuck or quiet, use these to prompt thinking out loud:

- "What are you looking for right now?"
- "What do you see on the screen?"
- "What were you expecting to see?"
- "What are you thinking?"
- "Why did you click/type that?"
- "How does this compare to what you thought would happen?"
- "Is there anything that's confusing right now?"

**DON'T:** Give instructions. If they're lost, ask "What would you try next?" or "Where do you think that button might be?"

## NOTES FOR THE TEST ADMIN (You)

**Do:**
- Be friendly and conversational
- Let silences happen — thinking takes time
- Write down observations in real-time (awkward moments, confusion points, smooth flows)
- Thank the tester genuinely at the end

**Don't:**
- Give hints or instructions
- Lead the tester toward a button or feature
- Suggest the "right" way to do something
- Interrupt their thinking process
- Disappear from the recording (keep your voice present)

**Red Flags to Note:**
- Tester searching for obvious buttons
- Tester confused by terminology
- Tester not understanding deck stats
- Tester unable to find reference card input
- Tester confused about formats (Commander vs. Standard)

**Good Signs to Note:**
- Tester quickly understands the interface
- Tester explains what they're doing unprompted
- Tester asks clarifying questions about the scenario (not the app)
- Tester notices deck differences between builds

## WHAT TO DO WITH THE RESULTS

After both test sessions:
1. **Watch the recordings** and note:
   - Where testers got confused
   - What was intuitive
   - What took longer than expected
   - Surprising behaviors or questions
2. **Identify patterns** between the two testers
3. **Prepare your findings** for discussion in Iteration 3 video

Key things to look for:
- Did they understand the app's purpose?
- Could they complete the scenarios without help?
- What was the hardest task?
- What felt seamless?
- What would they change?
