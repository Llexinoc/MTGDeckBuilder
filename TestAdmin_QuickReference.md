# ManaForge Think-Aloud Testing: Quick Reference Guide for Test Admin

**Print this and keep it handy during each test session**

---

## PRE-TEST CHECKLIST (15 min before)

- [ ] Recording software open and tested
- [ ] Microphone working (speak test: "Testing 1, 2, 3")
- [ ] Zoom/screen sharing ready
- [ ] App is loaded and working at http://127.0.0.1:5000/
- [ ] Scenario cards printed or ready to read
- [ ] Notepad for observations
- [ ] Timer or clock visible
- [ ] Phone on silent
- [ ] No distracting notifications

---

## DURING TEST: WHAT TO DO

### DO ✓
- Let them speak first (silence is okay)
- Ask open-ended questions: "What are you thinking?"
- Nod and listen actively
- Take notes on confusion/smooth moments
- Reassure them: "You're doing great"
- Ask follow-up: "What do you see?" "What did you expect?"
- Let them explore voluntarily
- Remember: testing the app, not them

### DON'T ✗
- Give step-by-step instructions
- Point to buttons or features
- Say "try clicking on..."
- Complete tasks for them
- Suggest the "right" way
- Fill silences too quickly
- Interrupt their thinking
- Ask leading questions ("Should you click here?")

---

## COMMON SITUATIONS & HOW TO RESPOND

| Situation | What They Say | Your Response |
|-----------|---------------|----------------|
| Stuck looking for button | "I don't know what to do" | "What are you looking for?" or "What would you try?" |
| Confused by term | "What's a bracket?" | "That's a good question — what do you think it might mean?" |
| Asks for help | "How do I...?" | "What would you try first?" |
| Goes quiet | *silence* | After 15 sec: "What are you thinking?" |
| Misunderstands task | Builds wrong thing | Let them finish, then ask clarifying Q about scenario |
| Success | Completes scenario | "Nice! What did you notice?" |

## SCENARIO CARD HANDOUTS

Print these and hand to participant during test:

---

### [PRINT & CUT] SCENARIO 1

**SCENARIO 1: Dragon Enthusiast**

You're relatively new to Magic. You've always thought dragons were cool, and your friend told you that you could build a dragon-themed Commander deck, but you have no idea where to start. There are 30,000+ Magic cards and you don't know which dragons are good or how to balance them.

You decide to try ManaForge to see if it can help you build a dragon deck quickly, without having to learn all the card rules first.

**Your Goal:** Use ManaForge to build a dragon-themed Commander deck. Once the deck is generated, spend a moment looking at what it created. Tell me: does it make sense? Do you see mostly dragon creatures?

---

### [PRINT & CUT] SCENARIO 2

**SCENARIO 2: Fun Over Winning**

The dragon deck you just built looks strong and competitive. But you remember your friend mentioning there's a way to adjust how serious a deck is. You want to build a dragon deck that's more casual and fun — the kind where goofy plays and theme matter more than winning, because you're playing with friends just for laughs.

**Your Goal:** Using ManaForge, build a dragon deck again, but this time adjust it to be more casual. Can you spot a difference between this casual version and the competitive one you built before?

---

### [PRINT & CUT] SCENARIO 3

**SCENARIO 3: Including a Card You Love**

You have a card you absolutely love — maybe it's a rare card you just pulled, or a card that means something to you. You want to make sure it's in your new deck. You've heard that some deckbuilding apps let you say "I want this card in my deck" before building.

You want to build a blue control deck, and you want to make sure it includes [Counterspell / your chosen card].

**Your Goal:** Build a blue control deck while adding a reference card. See if the app incorporates your preference into the final deck. Do you think your reference card ended up in the deck?

---

### [PRINT & CUT] SCENARIO 4

**SCENARIO 4: Learning How Decks Work**

You've built a few decks now and they look interesting, but you're curious — why did ManaForge choose these specific cards? What's the strategy? Why are there so many creatures vs. spells? Why these lands?

You want to understand the reasoning so you can learn how deckbuilding actually works.

**Your Goal:** Look at the deck details, the reasoning section, and the stats. Then explain back to me: What is this deck trying to do? Why does it have the cards it does? Don't worry if you don't understand everything — just share what you see.

---

### [PRINT & CUT] SCENARIO 5

**SCENARIO 5: Smaller Format, Faster Games**

Your usual playgroup plays Commander (100-card singleton), but next week you're going to a local game store's Friday Night Magic event, which uses Standard format (60-card constructed). The decks play faster and meaner.

You want to quickly adapt your dragon concept to work in this smaller, faster format.

**Your Goal:** Build a dragon deck in Standard format instead of Commander. What's different about this deck? Why do you think it's different?

## RED FLAGS 🚩

If you notice these, jot them down — they're important findings:

- [ ] Tester searches for obvious button (confusion about UI)
- [ ] Tester doesn't understand "theme description" concept
- [ ] Tester can't find text input box
- [ ] Tester confused by format dropdown
- [ ] Tester doesn't see deck output
- [ ] Tester doesn't understand what "reference cards" means
- [ ] Tester can't switch between formats
- [ ] Tester can't read deck reasoning/stats
- [ ] Tester gives up on a scenario
- [ ] Tester asks for help multiple times
- [ ] Tester says things are "confusing"

---

## GREEN LIGHTS ✓

If you notice these, also jot them down — these are good UX moments:

- [ ] Tester immediately understands the concept
- [ ] Tester thinks out loud unprompted
- [ ] Tester finds features without help
- [ ] Tester asks clarifying questions about scenario (not app)
- [ ] Tester clicks Build button confidently
- [ ] Tester reads reasoning/stats willingly
- [ ] Tester notices differences between deck versions
- [ ] Tester expresses interest ("Oh, cool!")
- [ ] Tester completes all scenarios smoothly
- [ ] Tester says they'd use this again

---

## AFTER EACH TEST

1. **Stop recording** — Thank them sincerely
2. **Export recording** as .mp4 immediately
3. **Write down notes** while fresh (within 5 min)
4. **Fill in observation sheet** with your highlights
5. **Review recording briefly** to catch anything you missed
6. **Timestamp key moments** if possible (e.g., "2:30 - confusion about bracket")

---

## INTERVIEW DEBRIEF QUESTIONS (while recording)

Read these at the end:

1. "Overall, what was your first impression of ManaForge?"
2. "Was there anything confusing or frustrating?"
3. "If you could suggest one improvement, what would it be?"
4. "Would you use this tool again?"

---

## COMMON PARTICIPANT QUESTIONS & NEUTRAL RESPONSES

| Question | Response |
|----------|----------|
| "Am I doing this right?" | "There's no 'right' way — just use it how feels natural" |
| "What should I type?" | "Whatever comes to mind — describe a deck you'd like to build" |
| "Is this correct?" | "What do you think?" |
| "Can I click this?" | "Try it and see what happens" |
| "Am I going too slow?" | "Go at your own pace — this isn't timed" |

---

**Remember:** You're testing the app, not the person. If something is confusing, that's valuable data!
